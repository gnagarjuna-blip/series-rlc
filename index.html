Numerical Problem

1. A series circuit has R = 10 Ω, L = 0.1 H, and C = 100 μF. Calculate the impedance and the power factor when connected to a 230 V, 50 Hz AC supply.

Given Data:
R = 10 Ω
L = 0.1 H
C = 100 μF = 100 × 10-6 F
V = 230 V
f = 50 Hz
Required Data:
Impedance Z = ?
Power Factor cosφ = ?
Formulas:
Inductive Reactance: XL = 2πfL
Capacitive Reactance: XC = 1 / (2πfC)
Impedance: Z = √(R2 + (XL - XC)2)
Power Factor: cosφ = R / Z
Solution:
A: Inductive Reactance (XL)
XL = 2 × 3.1416 × 50 × 0.1 = 31.42 Ω

B: Capacitive Reactance (XC)
XC = 1 / (2 × 3.1416 × 50 × 100 × 10-6) = 31.83 Ω

C: Total Impedance (Z)
Since XC > XL,
Difference = XC - XL = 31.83 - 31.42 = 0.41 Ω
Z = √(102 + 0.412) = 10.01 Ω

D: Calculate Power Factor (cosφ)
cosφ = 10 / 10.01 ≈ 0.999
Note: Because XC is slightly greater than XL, the power factor is leading.
