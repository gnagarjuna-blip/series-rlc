Part-B: Numerical Problems

Question: A series circuit has $R = 10 \, \Omega$, $L = 0.1 \, \text{H}$, and $C = 100 \, \mu\text{F}$. Calculate the impedance and the power factor when connected to a $230 \, \text{V}$, $50 \, \text{Hz}$ AC supply.

Given Parameters:
* Resistance ($R$) = $10 \, \Omega$
* Inductance ($L$) = $0.1 \, \text{H}$
* Capacitance ($C$) = $100 \, \mu\text{F} = 100 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{F}$
* Supply Voltage ($V$) = $230 \, \text{V}$
* Frequency ($f$) = $50 \, \text{Hz}$
Objective:
1. Total Impedance ($Z$) = ?
2. Power Factor ($\cos \phi$) = ?
Mathematical Formulas:
* Inductive Reactance: $X_L = 2\pi f L$
* Capacitive Reactance: $X_C = \frac{1}{2\pi f C}$
* Total Impedance: $Z = \sqrt{R^2 + (X_L - X_C)^2}$
* Power Factor: $\cos \phi = \frac{R}{Z}$
Step-by-Step Calculation:
A: Inductive Reactance ($X_L$)
$X_L = 2 \times 3.141 \times 50 \times 0.1 = 31.41 \, \Omega$

B: Capacitive Reactance ($X_C$)
$X_C = \frac{1}{2 \times 3.141 \times 50 \times 100 \times 10^{-6}} = 31.83 \, \Omega$

C: Total Impedance ($Z$)
$Z = \sqrt{10^2 + (31.41 - 31.83)^2}$
$Z = \sqrt{100 + (-0.42)^2} = \sqrt{100.176} \approx 10.01 \, \Omega$

D: Power Factor ($\cos \phi$)
$\cos \phi = \frac{10}{10.01} \approx 0.999$
Note: Since $X_C > X_L$, the circuit is slightly capacitive (Leading PF).

Part-A Short Answer Questions

1.a) What is an ideal voltage source?

An ideal voltage source is a two-terminal source whose voltage remains constant regardless of the current drawn. It has zero internal resistance ($R_i = 0$).
